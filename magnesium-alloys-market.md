<p><p>The magnesium alloys market comprises two main types: cast alloys and wrought alloys. Cast alloys are produced by pouring molten magnesium into molds, offering intricate shapes and designs, commonly used in automotive and aerospace applications. Wrought alloys, on the other hand, are formed through processes like rolling or forging, providing superior mechanical properties and strength, making them ideal for components requiring enhanced durability. Both types serve vital roles across various industries, driving innovations and applications in lightweight materials.</p></p>
