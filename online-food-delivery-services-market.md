<p><p>Online food delivery services can be categorized into two main market types: Restaurant-to-Consumer and Platform-to-Consumer. In the Restaurant-to-Consumer model, restaurants directly manage their own delivery processes, allowing them to maintain control over service quality and branding. In contrast, the Platform-to-Consumer model involves third-party platforms that connect consumers with various restaurants, facilitating orders and deliveries. This model offers a wide selection of cuisines but can sometimes lead to less control over the delivery experience from the restaurants' perspective.</p></p>
