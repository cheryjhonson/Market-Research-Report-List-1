<p><p>The oral cancer diagnosis market encompasses various imaging and diagnostic techniques. Computed Tomography (CT) Scan provides detailed cross-sectional images, aiding in tumor detection. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers high-contrast images of soft tissues, essential for assessing tumor extent. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) detects metabolic activity, useful for staging. Ultrasonography employs sound waves for real-time imaging. Biopsy confirms malignancy through tissue sampling. X-Ray provides basic imaging, while Endoscopy allows direct visualization. Fluorescence Imaging enhances detection through tissue fluorescence. Other methods include advanced imaging technologies and emerging diagnostic techniques.</p></p>
