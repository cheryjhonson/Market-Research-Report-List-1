<p><p>Gas Circuit Breakers (GCBs) are categorized based on voltage levels, including Medium Voltage (MV), High Voltage (HV), Extra High Voltage (EHV), and Ultra High Voltage (UHV). MV GCBs are used for distribution systems, while HV GCBs serve transmission networks. EHV GCBs handle voltages up to 765 kV and are employed in high-capacity power applications. UHV GCBs, operating above 765 kV, support crucial grid stability and long-distance transmission. Each type plays a vital role in ensuring reliable electrical infrastructure.</p></p>
