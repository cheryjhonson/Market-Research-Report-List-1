<p><p>The heat shield market comprises two primary types: rigid and flexible heat shields. Rigid heat shields are typically constructed from materials like ceramics or metals, offering high durability and strength, making them suitable for extreme environments, such as aerospace applications. In contrast, flexible heat shields are made from resilient materials like polymers or fabrics, allowing for adaptability and ease of installation in various configurations. Both types effectively mitigate heat transfer, catering to diverse industrial needs and applications.</p></p>
