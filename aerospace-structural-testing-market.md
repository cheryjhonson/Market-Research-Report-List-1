<p><p>The aerospace structural testing market is primarily divided into two categories: non-destructive testing (NDT) and destructive testing. NDT involves techniques that assess materials and components' integrity without causing any damage, ensuring safety and reliability throughout the product's lifecycle. Methods include ultrasonic testing, radiographic testing, and magnetic particle inspection. In contrast, destructive testing entails applying forces until failure to evaluate structural performance and material properties, providing critical data for design validation and safety assessments, essential in aerospace applications.</p></p>
