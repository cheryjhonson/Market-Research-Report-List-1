<p><p>The hip replacement implants market comprises three main types: metal, ceramic, and polyethylene. Metal implants are known for their strength and durability, often made from materials like titanium or cobalt-chromium, making them suitable for active patients. Ceramic implants offer excellent wear resistance and biocompatibility, reducing friction and wear, while also minimizing allergic reactions. Polyethylene implants, typically used as bearing surfaces, provide a low-friction interface but can wear over time. Each type addresses specific patient needs and preferences, influencing overall outcomes and longevity.</p></p>
